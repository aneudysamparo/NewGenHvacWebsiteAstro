Fire safety is a non-negotiable part of running a commercial kitchen. Grease buildup in hoods and
ducts is one of the most common causes of kitchen fires, which is why NFPA-96 sets the standard for
keeping exhaust systems clean and compliant.

## What NFPA-96 covers

NFPA-96 is the national standard for ventilation control and fire protection of commercial cooking
operations. In practice, it means your hoods, ducts, and exhaust systems need to be cleaned on a
schedule based on your cooking volume — and documented, so you can prove compliance to inspectors
and insurers.

## Why it matters

- **Fire prevention.** Removing flammable grease residue from your exhaust system dramatically
  reduces fire risk.
- **Compliance and insurance.** Inspectors and insurance companies expect documented, regular
  cleaning. Missing it can mean fines — or a denied claim.
- **Hygiene.** A clean exhaust system is part of a clean, professional kitchen.

## Certified cleaning

Certified hood cleaning uses industry-standard techniques to thoroughly clean exhaust systems and
remove dangerous grease, paired with documentation for your records. Grease containment solutions
also protect rooftops from damage and environmental hazards.
